VPT with Robots

Error-Rates Analysis: Full model

Joel Currie1, Katrina Louise McDonough2, Agnieszka Wykowska3, Maria Elena Giannaccini4, Patric Bach5

2024-12-17

Full Model:

Effects of Interest:

Self Theta:

The GLMM model detected a strong \(\theta_{self}\) effect (\(O.R = 38.2, CI = [15.7 - 93.1], p < .001\)), indicating that participants were more likely to make an error correctly identifying characters when they were oriented away from their own perspective, compared to when characters were facing towards them.

Other Perspective:

Crucially, our model did not identify a \(\theta_{other}\) effect (\(O.R = 1.18, CI = [0.76 - 1.83], p = .460\)). While error rates numerically showed the same pattern as RTs in our primary analysis, no statistically reliable differences were found indicating no presence of a speed/accuracy trade-off.

Other Fixed effects and Interactions:

Exposure:

Our model also detected a strong effect of Exposure (Experimental progress), demonstrating that as the experiment progressed participants made less errors: Full-model (\(O.R = 0.41, CI = [0.24 - 0.70], p = .001\))

Agent Category:

In addition, our full-model revealed a main effect of Agent Category when comparing the Human-Human group with the Human-Inanimate Robot group (\(O.R = 0.39, CI = [0.20 - 0.76], p = .005\)), and no difference between the Human-Animate Robot and Human-Inanimate Robot groups (\(p = .784\)),suggesting that participants made less mistakes when paired with another person, compared to being paired with either robot condition.

Experiment Progress * Agent Category

Our model detected an interaction between Exposure and Agent Category for the Human Group (\(O.R = 0.3, CI = [0.1 - 0.9], p = .027\)), compared to the Inanimate Robot group as baseline, this was not found when comparing the Animate Robot group to the Inanimate Robot group (\(p = .539\)). This tentatively suggests that as the experiment progressed, the rate at which people made errors in the Human group increased compared to when people were paired with the robot group. However, when visualized it seems that this reflects a floor effect, in that the Human group has reached an error rate minima.

Experiment Progress * Agent Category * Other Perspective

Finally, our model detected a small interaction between Experiment Progress, Agent Category and Other Perspective (\(O.R = 19.0, CI = [1.5 - 249.8], p = .025\)). This reflects that the Human group started with a strong disadvantage from the the other agents perspective, while this pattern of data inverted as the experiment progressed, while the Animate and Inanimate robot groups stayed static with respect to their initial conditions.
